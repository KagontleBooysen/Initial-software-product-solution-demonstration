3. Why lung cancer screening is important ?

Lung cancer screening is crucial for early detection, significantly increasing the chances of successful treatment and survival. Many people with lung cancer do not exhibit symptoms until the disease is advanced, making regular screening essential for high-risk individuals, such as smokers and those with a family history of lung disease. 7. 5 Signs of Lung Cancer

Lung cancer often presents with a variety of symptoms that can vary depending on the individual and the stage of the disease. Common signs include a persistent cough that worsens over time, chest pain that may be sharp or dull, and changes in the cough, such as coughing up blood or rust-colored sputum. Individuals may also experience shortness of breath, wheezing, and frequent respiratory infections. Unexplained weight loss, fatigue, and loss of appetite can also be significant indicators.

8. What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer is a major public health problem in the United States that causes approximately 25% of all cancer deaths. Smoking tobacco consumption is the leading cause of lung cancer which has a poor prognosis because only one out of every six patients survives for more than five years after receiving their diagnosis. Studies suggest that the consumption of smoke-tobacco related products has not been declining which means that the lung cancer epidemic will continue for years to come.
