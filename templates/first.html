Impilo an AI Device to Detect Early Lung Cancer Using Cough Analysis

Abstract



The project focuses on developing a machine-learning model that utilizes cough sound analysis for the early detection of lung cancer. While some studies suggest that coughing alone is not a reliable indicator of lung cancer, research has shown that cough analysis, combined with advanced machine-learning techniques, can be effective in identifying respiratory diseases. This project aims to gather diverse cough sound data from various demographic groups to train a machine-learning model optimized for detecting early signs of lung cancer. The trained model will be integrated into a user-friendly web application, enabling individuals to upload their cough sounds for analysis and receive a preliminary diagnosis within minutes. Rigorous testing and validation will be conducted to ensure the model's accuracy, with particular attention to minimizing false positives and negatives. Ethical approval and compliance with data protection standards will be obtained before deploying the platform for public or medical use. This non-invasive diagnostic tool has the potential to revolutionize early lung cancer detection, offering a cost-effective, accessible solution for individuals, especially in underserved communities.
